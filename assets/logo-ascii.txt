sir Logo — ASCII Art
====================

COMPACT (1 line)
Used in: status output, inline references
─────────────────────────────────────────

[sir]


BRACKETS (3 lines)
Used in: sir install, sir --version
─────────────────────────────────────────

[ · ? × ]
   sir

Used in: splash screen, about page
─────────────────────────────────────────

[         ]

[         ]
     sir
  sandbox in reverse


FULL (7 lines)
Used in: README, documentation
─────────────────────────────────────────

╔═══════════════════╗
║                   ║
║   ·    ?    ×     ║
║  allow ask deny   ║
║                   ║
╚═══════════════════╝
         sir

The brackets face outward — containment reversed. The three
dots are the verdict triad: allow (·), ask (?), deny (×).
Authority flows out of the lease; what's inside stays inside.

Wordmark rule: always lowercase. Never "Sir" or "SIR" in prose.
Terminal variant: [sir]  Bracketed variant: [ · ? × ] sir
